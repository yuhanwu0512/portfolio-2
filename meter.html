This project is for an independent study. The PHD student is working with NYSEG on a smart electric meter. NYSEG offered us with audio resources of focus groups and they talked about their opinions of smart meter. Our task is to analyze the data and design a smart meter which can meet users' needs.

Design Process

Code Transcripts & Themes

After transcribing the audio resources, we started to analyze them. We have three focus groups and the scripts are pretty long. So we didn't start directly from affinity diagram. Firstly, we used google drive comments to pick out useful information based on three questions:

1. What do people know about smart meters?
2.What are their concerns of smart meters?
3. Any other thing related to smart meters, sustainability, smart energy?

As shown in the screenshot above, we abbreviated three long scripts into key information in the comments. Then we integrated them into broader themes like: Concerns, Needs, common background and so on. During this process, we found that different focus groups have different topics and attitudes because they are from different areas in Ithaca: Northside, Southside and lansing. So we also investigated the demographics of the three areas. We can see that lansing is richer than northside and northside is richer than southside.

Affinity Diagram & Theme Trail

Based on the themes we came up with, we used affinity diagram to analyze them.

We connected themes in a same group and related them with demographics. Interestingly, we found some path in it. Southside cares more about fees and financial benefits and we call it "Money Trail". The northside cares more about life quality and we call it "Life quality trail". Lansing area talked more about technology so we call it "Technology reliability trail"

Ideation & Prototype

Our goal is to meet the needs of different groups of residents. So each of our team member worked individually to come up with 30 ideas.

After our discussion, teammates and mentor liked the way I use metaphor. Also, in order to meet more groups' needs, I decided to allow users choose different modes. People who care about fees can choose save money mode while people who care about environment can choose environment mode. For Saving Mode, there will be a saving jar. The more energy the user use, the less money will be left. For Environment Mode, there will be an ice cream. The more energy users use, the cute ice cream will melt more.
