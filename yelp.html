This is a UX case study and the App I choose to explore is Yelp.

People Problem Indentification

Problem I had

Everytime I decided to eat outside, I had difficulty choosing a restaurant. Although there are apps like yelp, the information is kind of overwhelming for me to make a decision.

Hypothesis

If yelp display the information in a more concise and organized way and make the information users care the most prominent, it will be easier to make a decision. E.g. reorganize the information in the filter function, add some personal recommendations.

User Research

I did 8 deep user interviews. Here are what I learned from user interviews:
      1. Most of them always think of using yelp when they decide to eat outside. They will use the filter functions according to their needs.
      2. When using the filter function, people care about distance, price, open now and rating most.
      3.Many people don't like the filter function in that there are too many items they don't care about and it takes effort to read them, some don't even make sense to them.
      4. Many people do not really trust the ratings or comments because different people have different criteria and tastes. The possibility that there might be fake data can also not be ignored.
      5. Some recommend that there should be personal recommendations so that the possibility that they will like the restaurant will be higher.

Market Research

I investigated Google Map and Foursquare as the market research. The biggest difference between yelp and these two is that Google Map and Foursquare has predetermined list. For example, after clicking "exploring around you", google map has a horizontal list of "breakfast, lunch, coffee, dinner, drinks". For each meal, there are categories like cheap eats, quick bites and so on. This predetermined list starts the journey of finding a restaurant from choosing the scenerio which can not be easier. The whole system offers an impression that by answering the simplest question passively, you will be able to find the most suitable restaurant. This also responds to the findings in the user interviews that people want quick access and simplified information.

Screenshot of Google Map & Foursquare

Identifying the people problem

People want to find places to eat which can meet their needs quickly. They want the places can be trustful and satisfying. They don't like the filter function in yelp because the items are overwhelming and the ratings and comments are not 100 percent trustful.

Solutions

According to all the user researches, I found that users prefer answering objective questiosns to making subjective choices.

Brainstorm

From the brainstorm, I decided that the new filter should be more personalized. Users just need to make objective choices and the following items will be based on the previous choices.
